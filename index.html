Graphic and Online Design

May 2005 – May 2007

I went from being in the Sciences to being in the Liberal Arts. In Lock Haven's "GOLD" program, I developed an appreciation of basic design principles and typography. The most valuable thing I took away from this program was a very serious eye towards our own design work.

For each assignment (like magazine articles or logos), we were given a few days to work on each piece. But "turning in" your work involved standing at the front of the class, presenting your piece, and standing nearby while your classmates ruthlessly tore into your design. You would hear that your piece was ineffective at its goals, or that you didn't use space efficiently, or that it "just plain sucked". It was an incredibly humbling experience, and gave me a fantastic appreciation of the amount of work it took to make something effective. I wouldn't trade those experiences for any other experience in the world.
